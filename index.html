Gaming ecosystem
Skill-based · not gambling

Games of skill, settled fairly

PKC is built around games where winning comes from strategy and skill — the way chess or go has always worked. That design choice keeps the ecosystem on the right side of the law and rewards real players.

  • Tournament entry

    Use PKC to enter skill tournaments. Prize pools reward the best players, not the luckiest.

  • Skill rewards

    Earn PKC by winning ranked matches. Ratings and results are transparent and verifiable.

  • In-game items

    Buy boards, piece sets, and cosmetics in the marketplace — pure utility, no wagering.

Collectibles
Utility collectibles — not a financial return

NFTs for the board, not for speculation

Every NFT in this ecosystem does something in-game or proves a real result — cosmetics, achievements, and access. None of them pay dividends, and none are sold as random "mystery box" draws.

Cosmetic

Board & piece sets

Digital art for boards and piece styles. Purely cosmetic — they change nothing about how a game is won.

Achievement

Champion badges

Minted automatically to verified winners of skill tournaments. A permanent, on-chain record of a real result — not something you can buy.

Access

Membership pass

Unlocks entry to select tournaments and features. Utility access only — holding one is not an investment and pays no return.

The game plan

Roadmap, one move at a time

Ordered by priority, starting with the things that can be verified on-chain and cost little or nothing.

Move 1 · Now

Foundations of trust

Publish a multi-sig treasury (Gnosis Safe), verify the contract source publicly, and run automated security pre-checks. All achievable at near-zero cost.

Move 2 · Next

Lock & audit

Lock liquidity with proof on-chain, then complete a full third-party smart-contract audit once budget allows. Publish every report.

Move 3

Board-game arena launch

Open the first skill-based tournaments with PKC entry and rewards, plus transparent ratings and match history.

Move 4

NFT collectibles launch

Introduce board & piece-set cosmetics, plus champion badges minted automatically to verified tournament winners — on-chain proof of a real result.

Move 5

Marketplace & listings

Launch the item and NFT marketplace, and apply to CoinMarketCap and CoinGecko — a step that requires audit and lock proofs to be public first.

Move 6

Membership & community governance

Introduce a membership-pass NFT for tournament access, and open limited community voting on tournament rules to PKC and NFT holders.

Before you act

Risk & legal disclosure

Written plainly, on purpose.

Not financial advice

Nothing here is an offer, solicitation, or recommendation to buy or hold PKC Coin, nor investment, legal, or tax advice. Evaluate any decision independently or with a licensed advisor.

Skill-based gaming, not gambling

The PKC gaming ecosystem is designed around games of skill where outcomes are decided by strategy — not chance. PKC is a utility token for entry fees, skill rewards, and in-game items. It is not a wagering or gambling instrument. Where you live, confirm that participating is lawful before taking part.

Key risks

  • PKC's price is volatile and can fall to zero. Staking rewards are variable, not guaranteed.
  • The contract has not completed a full third-party audit. Unaudited code carries higher risk.
  • Liquidity is not yet locked, so it is technically possible for liquidity to be removed.
  • The team currently uses private, escrow-based KYC rather than public disclosure — treat this as materially higher risk than a fully public team.
  • Regulatory treatment of cryptoassets and online gaming varies by country and can change.
